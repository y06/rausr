### Why In-Person Exhibitions Refuse to Disappear

Virtual events solve convenience. Exhibitions solve something harder: **trust at scale**.

In real halls, your audience gets:
- **multi-sensory proof** (touch, weight, materials, sound, speed)
- **social proof** (who else is here, who queues, who stops)
- **context** (how your product compares when competitors are 20 meters away)
- **human calibration** (tone, micro-signals, sincerity)

{{< quote >}}
Digital can explain. Physical can convince.
{{< /quote >}}

#### Hidden mechanism: ‚ÄúCostly signaling‚Äù
Economists use this term for actions that are expensive enough to be meaningful.
Showing up to an exhibition (travel, booth cost, logistics, time) signals:
- you‚Äôre stable enough to invest
- you‚Äôre confident enough to be compared live
- you expect to be around after the show

That‚Äôs why real-world presence often accelerates trust faster than weeks of emails.

### How to Use an Exhibition to Launch a New Product or Service

If you‚Äôre introducing something new, exhibitions can give you the fastest feedback loop you‚Äôll ever get ‚Äî if you design for it.

#### Before the show: build a ‚Äúconversation architecture‚Äù
- Define your **ideal visitor** (industry, role, pain point, budget range)
- Decide one measurable goal: meetings booked, partner intros, pilot signups
- Prepare 2 versions of the pitch: **10 seconds** and **60 seconds**
- Make the demo physically obvious: screens are fine, but *interaction* is better
- Plan follow-up paths: ‚Äúbook a call‚Äù, ‚Äúpilot request‚Äù, ‚Äúpricing‚Äù, ‚Äúpartner program‚Äù

#### During the show: optimize for quality, not volume
- Make it easy to approach: open corners, clear entry, no ‚Äúsales wall‚Äù
- Use a simple question that qualifies politely: ‚ÄúWhat are you trying to improve this year?‚Äù
- Capture context, not just contacts: role + problem + timeline + next step

#### After the show: speed decides ROI
Follow up in 24‚Äì72 hours with:
- one recap sentence (‚Äúyou said X, so here is Y‚Äù)
- one proof asset (case, demo link, spec)
- one clear next step (calendar link, proposal, sample)
